Research

How research is done in the department

Research in the department of computer science is driven by the curiosity and creativity of the faculty and students, who work on various aspects of computing and its applications. The department has a strong culture of collaboration, both within and outside the university, and encourages interdisciplinary research that can address the grand challenges of our time. The department also fosters a close relationship with the industry, and provides opportunities for students to engage in real-world projects and internships.

The department has several research groups, each focusing on a specific area of computer science, such as artificial intelligence, machine learning, data science, cybersecurity, software engineering, computer networks, computer graphics, human-computer interaction, and more. Each research group is led by one or more faculty members, and consists of graduate students, undergraduate students, and sometimes postdoctoral fellows and visiting scholars. The research groups meet regularly to discuss their ongoing research, share ideas, and provide feedback to each other. The research groups also organize seminars, workshops, and conferences to disseminate their research findings and interact with other researchers in the field.

The department offers various research degrees, such as MPhil, MSc, and PhD, as well as research-oriented courses for undergraduate students. The research degrees require students to conduct original and independent research under the supervision of a faculty member, and to write and defend a thesis or dissertation. The research-oriented courses allow undergraduate students to participate in research projects, learn research methods and skills, and write research papers or reports. The department also supports students to publish their research in reputable journals and conferences, and to apply for scholarships and grants to fund their research.

Past and present research activities

The department of computer science has a long and rich history of research, dating back to its establishment in 1974. Over the years, the department has produced many notable research contributions and achievements, such as:

  • Developing the first computer network in Ghana, connecting the University of Ghana to other universities and research institutions in the country.
  • Creating the first Ghanaian language software, enabling users to type and process text in Akan, Ewe, Ga, and Hausa.
  • Designing and implementing the first electronic voting system in Ghana, which was used in the 2008 presidential and parliamentary elections.
  • Building the first supercomputer in West Africa, which was ranked among the top 500 supercomputers in the world in 2013.
  • Winning several awards and recognitions, such as the Google Faculty Research Award, the Microsoft Research PhD Fellowship, the ACM SIGKDD Best Paper Award, the IEEE Computer Society Richard E. Merwin Scholarship, and more.

The department continues to pursue excellence in research, and is currently working on various cutting-edge and impactful research projects, such as:

  • Developing machine learning and natural language processing techniques to analyze social media data and extract useful insights for various domains, such as health, education, politics, and disaster management.
  • Exploring cybersecurity and privacy issues and solutions for emerging technologies, such as cloud computing, internet of things, blockchain, and smart contracts.
  • Creating computer graphics and virtual reality applications to enhance the learning and teaching of science, technology, engineering, and mathematics (STEM) subjects.
  • Investigating human-computer interaction and user experience design methods and tools to improve the usability and accessibility of digital products and services.
  • Collaborating with other disciplines and sectors, such as agriculture, medicine, energy, and environment, to apply computer science solutions to real-world problems.
