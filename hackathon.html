Hackathon

UG Hacks 2023

UG Hacks 2023 is the annual hackathon organized by the Department of Computer Science, in collaboration with the Computer Science Students Association and other partners. The hackathon is open to all students of the University of Ghana, regardless of their major or level of experience. The hackathon aims to provide a platform for students to showcase their creativity, innovation, and problem-solving skills, as well as to network with other students, mentors, and industry experts.

The theme of UG Hacks 2023 is "Sustainable Development Goals". The hackathon challenges students to use technology to address one or more of the 17 Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs) set by the United Nations, such as ending poverty, ensuring quality education, promoting gender equality, combating climate change, and more. The hackathon will take place from January 24 to January 26, 2023, at the Department of Computer Science. The hackathon will have various workshops, talks, and activities to help students learn new skills, get inspired, and have fun. The hackathon will also have prizes for the best teams and projects, as well as certificates and swag for all participants.

Past Hackathons

The Department of Computer Science has been organizing hackathons since 2018, with different themes and partners. Here are some of the past hackathons that we have hosted:

  • UG Hacks 2022: The theme of UG Hacks 2022 was "Digital Transformation". The hackathon challenged students to use technology to transform various sectors and industries, such as health, education, agriculture, finance, and more. The hackathon was sponsored by Meta, Google, IBM, and MTN. The hackathon had over 200 participants, and the winning team created a smart irrigation system using IoT and AI.
  • UG Hacks 2021: The theme of UG Hacks 2021 was "Social Innovation". The hackathon challenged students to use technology to create social impact and positive change, such as improving social justice, enhancing civic engagement, promoting social inclusion, and more. The hackathon was sponsored by Microsoft, Amazon, Huawei, and Vodafone. The hackathon had over 150 participants, and the winning team created a chatbot that provides mental health support and resources.
  • UG Hacks 2020: The theme of UG Hacks 2020 was "Smart Cities". The hackathon challenged students to use technology to make cities more livable, sustainable, and resilient, such as improving urban mobility, reducing environmental pollution, increasing public safety, and more. The hackathon was sponsored by IBM, Google, Cisco, and Airtel. The hackathon had over 100 participants, and the winning team created a smart parking system using IoT and cloud computing.
  • UG Hacks 2019: The theme of UG Hacks 2019 was "Artificial Intelligence". The hackathon challenged students to use AI to solve various problems and tasks, such as image recognition, natural language processing, speech synthesis, and more. The hackathon was sponsored by Google, Microsoft, IBM, and MTN. The hackathon had over 80 participants, and the winning team created a voice assistant that can answer questions and perform tasks.
  • UG Hacks 2018: The theme of UG Hacks 2018 was "Blockchain". The hackathon challenged students to use blockchain to create decentralized applications and solutions, such as smart contracts, cryptocurrency, digital identity, and more. The hackathon was sponsored by IBM, Microsoft, Meta, and Vodafone. The hackathon had over 60 participants, and the winning team created a blockchain-based voting system.

Famous Hackathons in Africa

Hackathons are not only popular in Ghana, but also in other African countries. Here are some of the famous hackathons that have been held or are planned to be held in Africa:

  • AfricaHacks: AfricaHacks is a platform that connects African hackers, innovators, and entrepreneurs with opportunities and resources to create solutions for Africa. AfricaHacks organizes various hackathons, workshops, and events across the continent, such as NaijaHacks, AfroHacks, and AfricaHacks Global. AfricaHacks also provides mentorship, training, and funding for the best teams and projects.
  • Hack for Africa: Hack for Africa is a series of hackathons that aim to foster innovation and collaboration among African youth, and to address the challenges and opportunities of the African continent. Hack for Africa has organized hackathons in various countries, such as Kenya, Nigeria, Rwanda, and South Africa, with themes such as education, health, agriculture, and energy.
  • Pan-African Robotics Competition: Pan-African Robotics Competition (PARC) is an annual robotics competition that brings together students from different African countries to showcase their skills and creativity in robotics and STEM. PARC has different categories and challenges for different age groups, such as robot soccer, robot maze, robot art, and robot dance. PARC also provides workshops, seminars, and exhibitions to inspire and educate the participants and the public.
